## Integration Points

### Strategy Selection Framework:
1. **Identify Business Stage:** Startup → Growth → Scale → Mature
2. **Assess Available Resources:** Budget, team, time
3. **Evaluate Market Dynamics:** Competitive landscape, customer preferences
4. **Choose Mix of Strategies:**
   - 1-2 "Beachhead" strategies (fast wins)
   - 2-3 "Foundation" strategies (long-term moats)
   - 2-3 "Scaling" strategies (growth drivers)
   - 1-2 "Leverage" strategies (compound effects)

### Example Strategic Combinations:

**SaaS Company (Growth Stage):**
- Beachhead: Freemium + Webinars (faster initial traction)
- Foundation: Content + Community (long-term moat)
- Scaling: Land & Expand + Free-to-Paid optimization
- Leverage: Referral program + API ecosystem

**Services Company (Startup):**
- Beachhead: Referral Program (leverage existing network)
- Foundation: Thought Leadership blog (build authority)
- Scaling: Productized Services (increase margins)
- Leverage: Group Coaching (3-10x leverage)

**Real Estate Agent (Individual):**

- Foundation: Neighborhood marketing (hyper-local authority)
- Scaling: Investor partnerships (higher deal size)

## Key Success Factors Across All Strategies

### Universal Success Factors:
1. **Clear Value Communication:** Every strategy depends on articulating value
2. **Execution Excellence:** Good strategy + mediocre execution = failure
3. **Measurement & Iteration:** Data-driven optimization is essential
4. **Customer Insights:** Deep understanding of customer pain/desires
5. **Timing:** Right strategy at right time to market
6. **Resource Commitment:** Adequate budget and team dedication
7. **Patience with Compound Effects:** Most strategies take 6-12 months

### Common Mistakes Across Verticals:
1. **Spreading Too Thin:** Attempting 10 strategies instead of 3-4 focused
2. **No Measurement:** Implementing without tracking results
3. **Inconsistent Execution:** Starting strategies and abandoning too early
4. **Wrong Customer Segment:** Mismatched strategy to target market
5. **Ignoring Competitive Response:** Assuming no competitive reaction
6. **Underinvestment:** Expecting results without adequate resource commitment
7. **Overreliance on Single Channel:** All eggs in one basket
